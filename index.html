About Us

Thulir, an initiative of the Tiruvallur District Administration is dedicated to ending child marriage. We are committed to protecting the rights of children, especially girls, and ending the harmful practice of child marriage worldwide.
Thulir, which means sprout or shoot, indicates the growth and development. Thereby, focusing the growth and development of young children, especially girls who are at risk of being forced into marriage at a young age. Child marriage is a widespread social issue in many parts of the world, and it is a violation of children's rights, particularly girls.
Thulir seeks to protect children's rights, particularly girls. It is a movement that recognizes that child marriage is not just a cultural practice but a human rights violation. Child marriage is a practice that affects millions of girls around the world, particularly in developing countries. These girls are forced to leave school and marry at a young age, often to much older men. This robs them of their childhood and exposes them to a lifetime of abuse, poverty, and poor health.
Through our various programs and initiatives, we aim to empower girls and their communities to say no to child marriage.
We believe that education, empowerment, and community engagement are key to ending this harmful practice.

Our programs include:

Education:

We provide education and life skills training to girls, so they can acquire the knowledge and skills they need to make informed decisions about their lives.

Community Engagement:

We work with local communities, parents, and activists to raise awareness about the harms of child marriage and promote alternative cultural practices that empower girls.

Advocacy and Policy Change:

We advocate for policy changes at the district level to protect girls from child marriage and ensure their rights are respected.

Legal Support:

We provide legal support and assistance to girls and their families to help them navigate the legal system and protect their rights.

Health and Well-being:

We promote access to health services and provide support to ensure the health and well-being of girls and their families.
We believe that every child deserves the right to a safe and healthy childhood, free from the harmful practices of child marriage.
Join us in our efforts to create a world where children can thrive and reach their full potential. Every girl has the right to live a happy, healthy, and fulfilling life. Together, we can make a difference and create a world where child marriage is a thing of the past.

Vision

Our vision is to create a district where every girl and boy is empowered to exercise their rights and make informed decisions, without the threat of child marriage, and with equal access to education, healthcare, skills development, and livelihood opportunities. We envision a community where every young person is equipped with the tools and resources they need to realize their aspirations and achieve their full potential, building a brighter and more equitable future for themselves and their families.


Mission

  • To provide comprehensive education and skills training for girls and boys, equipping them with the knowledge and tools necessary to succeed in the modern world.
  • To raise awareness about the harms of child marriage and promote alternatives that prioritize the health, safety, and wellbeing of young people.
  • To establish and maintain accessible and affordable healthcare services that meet the needs of girls and boys, including reproductive and sexual health services.
  • To work with community leaders, parents, and guardians to challenge harmful gender norms and promote gender equality, creating a more inclusive and supportive environment for all young people.
  • To foster partnerships with employers, businesses, and other stakeholders to create pathways to decent work and livelihood opportunities for young people, including those from marginalized communities.
  • To promote youth leadership and participation in decision-making, ensuring that the voices and perspectives of young people are heard and valued in all aspects of community life.
  • To monitor progress, evaluate impact, and continuously adapt our strategies and approaches to meet the evolving needs and priorities of young people in the district.
